Hi, I'm Alex!

I'm a recent PhD graduate from the Department of Geosciences at Princeton University, where I worked with Frederik Simons and Jessica Irving. Before attending Princeton I studied Earth Sciences at UC San Diego.

My research work focused on using earthquakes to improve our understanding of the interior structure of the Earth. Specifically, we were interested in a region 400 to 700 kilometers below the Earth's surface known as the mantle transition zone. This region behaves like a thermostat, allowing us to measure the internal temperature of the planet. By studying its characteristics in different locations, we hope to gain a deeper understanding of the forces shaping the Earth's landscapes today.

This work integrated aspects of geology, physics, math, and computer science - I was never very good at picking a favorite subject. I hope you find the contents of my website interesting and informative. Feel free to reach out if you would like to get in touch!
